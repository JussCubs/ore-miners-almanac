**Sentiment read:** playful, chaotic, and constructive. The community is turning miner stories into reusable education while keeping the mineshaft personality intact. Thep explicitly encouraged more stories because every bit of feedback is being considered for mining content.

**Notable miner story — FiL:**

FiL framed ORE as a relief from information overload and memecoin emotional whiplash. Old GPU mining felt too complex to operate alone; memecoins created a euphoria/pain loop. ORE feels different because the mining loop is understandable, accessible, and rewards patience.

Key ideas to preserve:

- ORE's simplicity is not a weakness — it is what makes mining accessible to normal people.
- Patience is a psychological edge as much as a protocol mechanic.
- Small deployments can keep a miner participating without turning the session into panic.
- Motherlode chasing is tempting but should be treated as high-risk, especially if self-control is shaky.
- ore.fyi remains a recurring checkpoint for whether current mining cost makes sense.

**Strategy insight extracted from FiL:**

- Low SOL mode: tiny deploys across ~19 blocks.
- Higher conviction / more SOL mode: 25 blocks with controlled sizing.
- Motherlode chase mode: if doing it at all, step down size as SOL drains instead of escalating emotionally.
- Solo-seeking mode: avoid the immediate post-motherlode window if high-ballers appear to be crowding the board.
- Timing observation: some European weekend/Sunday-night windows may feel quieter, but this belongs in the “watch conditions” bucket rather than hard doctrine.
