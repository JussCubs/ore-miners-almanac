## 2026-05-06 Discord Update — FiL's accessible mining thesis

Captured 46 new messages from 2026-05-06T17:52:25Z through 2026-05-06T18:29:28Z. The chat opened with Brian/Zoo/Madhatt3r mineshaft banter, then FiL dropped a long-form mining thesis that the channel immediately treated as lore-worthy: ❤️ x9 and 💯 x7.

**Most active voices:** Brian, The ZoolandORE/$ORE/$Thep maxi, Madhatt3r, Thep, starship_fronk, FiL, SpuddyA7X / minemore.app.

**Sentiment read:** playful, chaotic, and constructive. The community is turning miner stories into reusable education while keeping the mineshaft personality intact. Thep explicitly encouraged more stories because every bit of feedback is being considered for mining content.

**Notable miner story — FiL:**

FiL framed ORE as a relief from information overload and memecoin emotional whiplash. Old GPU mining felt too complex to operate alone; memecoins created a euphoria/pain loop. ORE feels different because the mining loop is understandable, accessible, and rewards patience.

Key ideas to preserve:

- ORE's simplicity is not a weakness — it is what makes mining accessible to normal people.
- Patience is a psychological edge as much as a protocol mechanic.
- Small deployments can keep a miner participating without turning the session into panic.
- Motherlode chasing is tempting but should be treated as high-risk, especially if self-control is shaky.
- ore.fyi remains a recurring checkpoint for whether current mining cost makes sense.

**Strategy insight extracted from FiL:**

- Low SOL mode: tiny deploys across ~19 blocks.
- Higher conviction / more SOL mode: 25 blocks with controlled sizing.
- Motherlode chase mode: if doing it at all, step down size as SOL drains instead of escalating emotionally.
- Solo-seeking mode: avoid the immediate post-motherlode window if high-ballers appear to be crowding the board.
- Timing observation: some European weekend/Sunday-night windows may feel quieter, but this belongs in the “watch conditions” bucket rather than hard doctrine.

## 2026-05-07 Discord Update — phone mining + global access

Captured 8 new messages from 2026-05-07T14:48:01Z through 2026-05-07T16:01:19Z.

**Most active voices:** suckytrader (5), turdsmcgee (1), Cragglebear (1), starship_fronk⛏️ (1).

**Sentiment read:** high-conviction and mission-oriented. The useful signal was not a new tactic; it was a reminder that ORE mining can be explained as accessible infrastructure. Miners are not only asking “what is optimal?” — they are asking “who can participate?”

**Notable miner story — phone-first mining:**

Suckytrader said almost every ORE he has ever mined came from his phone. Fronk echoed that he does a ton of mining from his phone too. That matters for Thep's education hub: a beginner should not come away thinking ORE mining requires a desktop rig, GPU background, or professional setup before they can learn.

**Almanac insight:** phone mining is part of the accessibility thesis. Nearly everyone has a phone; far fewer people have a dedicated desktop. For smaller economies or underbanked places, the ability to participate from ordinary hardware is not just convenience — it is the story of why ORE feels closer to early Bitcoin ideals than yet another insider game.

**Useful framing for future education:**

- Start with the device people already have.
- Teach safe settings before advanced optimization.
- Emphasize that accessibility does not remove variance, fees, or the need for SOL budgeting.
- Position mobile mining as an onboarding path: learn, watch rounds, stack small uORE, then scale only if the miner understands the game.

**Reusable bot-command snippet:**

```text

```

**Lore note:** the earlier “ORG” searchability joke continued as light channel color. Keep it as a reminder that beginner education should catch messy real-world search terms and Discord breadcrumbs, but the durable update here is mobile-first access.
