## !lowandslow

**Source:** Fronk

> Mine with all 25 tiles. Check ore.fyi production cost before every session. If it costs more to mine than to buy, don't mine. Keep your miner small. Be consistent. Be patient. This isn't a sprint.

**When to use:** When someone asks "how should I set up my miner?"

## !noclaim

**Source:** Community consensus

> Don't claim your uORE. It earns ~70-75% APR just sitting there. Claiming costs a 10% fee. Patient holders absorb the fees that impatient claimers pay. The longer you hold, the more you earn. If you must claim, wait at least 1.5 months so the yield offsets the fee.

**When to use:** When someone asks "should I claim my uORE?"

---

## !tiles

**Source:** Community consensus

> Mine all 25 tiles. Every round. No exceptions. 25 tiles = mining. Fewer tiles = gambling. The math favors consistency over luck. You don't need to hit a motherlode to win — you need to stack uORE over time.

**When to use:** When someone asks "how many tiles should I mine?"

---

## !safedefaults

**Source:** Brian

> Before you mine: set your tile count to 25, set a SOL budget you can afford to lose, and understand every setting before you click start. Misconfigured miners can blow all your SOL in one round. Safe defaults aren't optional — they're how you survive long enough to become a veteran.

**When to use:** When a new miner is setting up for the first time

---

## !multiwall

**Source:** Suckytrader

> If you need liquidity, use multiple wallets. Mine into several, claim from one while the others keep compounding yield. This way you get access to some ORE without sacrificing your entire compounding position.

**When to use:** When someone needs liquidity but doesn't want to destroy their yield

---

## !whymine

**Source:** Cubs / Community

> Mining ORE isn't about winning every round. It's about building a position in the only asset you can't fully buy your way into: uORE. The edge is patience, consistency, and information. Stack uORE, let yield compound, and let time do the work.

**When to use:** When someone asks "why mine ORE instead of just buying it?"
